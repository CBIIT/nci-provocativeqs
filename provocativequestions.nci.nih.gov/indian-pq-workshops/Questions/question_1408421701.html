Question ID: WS-9
Submitted by: Sankara Reddiar Kannan
August 19, 2014

Oral cancer in young (<45 years) patients â€“ Is a new entity? Usually, oral cancer is a disease mainly affecting the older age group. This has generally been attributed to indiscriminate substance abuse, particularly the use of smokeless tobacco and related products, over a considerable period of time. Recent epidemiological data on oral cancer shows, there is an increase in the incidence rate of oral cancer, especially in tongue, in young adults (<45 Years) and most of them does not have any oral habits. Also the current data revealed that there is a significant difference in the survival pattern of young patients having carcinoma of Tongue. The reasons for this are largely uncertain and the explanations offered are mostly speculative. The changes in risk factor and age dependence with the incidence and prognosis of oral cancer implied a possibility of change in the pathway/s of carcinogenesis. Thus, it arises following pertinent queries to know further and manage the disease properly. Is it a new entity? What will be the epidemiological and genetic etiological factors for this group? Is, the carcinogenetic pathway for this entity is different from the usual form of oral cancers?

We have to be careful in coding primary site of cancer oral vs. oro-pharyngeal where the risk factors, disease biology and management are different. If most of the tongue cancers are base of the tongue then, it is oro-pharyngeal and the factors are different which can be explained for occurrence in young patients. But identifying primary site (oral tongue vs. base) can be challenging in advanced stages which adds to the confusion. This question merits to be explored and answered appropriately which can add significant knowledge to the evidence existing especially in India.
